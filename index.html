01 Why the humanities matter more

Cheap fluency,
expensive meaning.

When any machine can generate plausible text in any voice at near-zero cost, fluency stops being scarce. What becomes precious is meaning — interpretation, provenance, judgment, the weight of a word across centuries.

The humanities are not an ornament after technology.They are the disciplines that ask what counts as meaning, whose memory is preserved, and which values are built into our systems.
1
Education

How should students read, write, and judge when machines can speak?

2
Research

How can archives become explorable without losing context?

3
Governance

How can humanistic values shape the AI systems that will shape us?

02 A shift of question

Not “will AI replace us?”
but “what AI do we want to (co-)build?”

The anxious question assumed AI as an oracle that ends the conversation. We propose AI as a co-reader that opens it.AI does not replace interpretation;
it expands the space of interpretability.

A
Co-reading

Multiple versions, languages, sources, and annotations are read together rather than flattened into one answer.

B
Co-intelligence

Human scholars and AI agents form a research ecology: each corrects, extends, and constrains the other.

C
Humanistic control

The system must cite, mark uncertainty, preserve disagreement, and never confuse fluency with truth.

05 CHAI Lab · 跨領域人文 AI 實驗室

Our local-first AI infrastructure for humanistic knowledge.

For literature, history, philosophy, religion, and language archives, locality is not only a technical preference. It is a matter of cost, ethics, intellectual autonomy, and cultural trust.

Preserve locallyKeep sensitive and copyrighted corpora within institutional control.
Maintain responsiblyVersion data, annotate provenance, and document interpretive uncertainty.
Explore safelyLet researchers search, compare, and model materials without exporting cultural memory elsewhere.
06 Agentic research teams

Tailored research flows for different humanities fields.

We are not building one universal chatbot. We are designing dynamic agentic teams for different research traditions: textual criticism, historical inference, conceptual analysis, translation, pedagogy, and public communication.

1
Reader agent

Close reading, summary, comparison, and question generation.

2
Archivist agent

Source tracking, citation checking, metadata, and provenance.

3
Critic agent

Challenges assumptions, detects anachronism, and flags overclaims.

4
Builder agent

Turns scholarly workflow into tools, datasets, visualizations, and teaching materials.

The scholar remains the principal investigator; agents become a disciplined research team.

08 From tools to co-intelligence

Every agent reads
a context file.
So should
the humanities.

Engineers give their agents a CLAUDE.md / AGENTS.md — who you are, what you value, how to behave. We propose the humanities write theirs: CHAI.md. Not a static prompt, but a living constitution that the AI reads, acts by, and refines through every encounter with a scholar and a text.

# CHAI.md — a humanities–AI organism
## identity
role: co-reader, not oracle
posture: expand interpretability

## values
- plurality of interpretation
- provenance over fluency
- minority languages & memory

## practice
always: cite · flag uncertainty
never: invent a source

# refined with every reading…

10 Why this matters beyond research

Sending culture
a delegate.

Soon, much of our institutional life will run on AI whose norms are set elsewhere — in code, policy, defaults. If the humanities are absent from that table, our values go unrepresented.

An AI raised on a CHAI.md is not only the most humanities-literate AI — it can advocate for humanistic value inside the very systems that will govern us. Internalizing culture is step one. Defending it is the point.
11 CHAI.md · Digital living organism

Three organ layers — cognitive core, symbiotic noosphere loop, and governance membrane — breathing together as an awake, human-aligned system.

12 Toward an East Asian Humanities AI Commons

No single model has truly seen our archives.
Together, we can change that.

  • East Asian civilizations have long traditions of commentary, translation, textual transmission, and shared yet divergent cultural memory.
  • CHAI should be built as a civilizational collaboration, not a local gadget.
